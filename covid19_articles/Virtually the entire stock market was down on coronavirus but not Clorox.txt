Virtually the entire stock market was down on coronavirus ... but not Clorox

general
 Stocks fell to their lowest levels in months Monday on the spread of coronavirus. But the companies that make your cleaning products are rallying. Shares of Clorox ( CLX ) , Procter & Gamble ( PG ) , Colgate Palmolive ( CL ) and Kimberly-Clark ( KMB ) were all up Monday. An outbreak can be good for disinfectant sales: In 2009, when consumers were worried about the spread of swine flu, hand sanitizer sales spiked 70% over a six-month stretch, according to Nielsen . But the stocks werent necessarily rising because investors expect them to sell a ton of cleaning supplies. These companies are defensive stocks, and investors often head to them during volatile periods. "Its a rotational, get defensive sort of day," said Kevin Grundy, who covers the consumer products sector at Jefferies. More than 450 S&P 500 stocks were down Monday.
